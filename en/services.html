Arbitration – A Fast and Confidential Solution for Business Disputes

In business, disputes can pose significant financial risks and disrupt operations. Arbitration offers an efficient and expedient way to resolve conflicts without the prolonged timelines of traditional litigation.

Read more

In arbitration, the parties agree that any disputes will be handled by an arbitration tribunal rather than a court. This process provides several advantages:

  • Speed – Arbitration typically takes a few months to a year, whereas court proceedings can last for several years.
  • Confidentiality – Unlike public court cases, arbitration remains strictly confidential, protecting trade secrets and business reputations.
  • Expertise – Parties can select arbitrators with specialized knowledge in the relevant legal or business field, ensuring well-informed decision-making.
  • Finality – Arbitration awards are binding and are often enforced more swiftly than court rulings.

We provide comprehensive support throughout the arbitration process, including:

  • Drafting and negotiating arbitration agreements
  • Legal advice and strategic planning for the arbitration process
  • Representation in arbitration proceedings
  • Enforcement of arbitration awards

Arbitration is particularly suitable for commercial contract disputes, where parties wish to avoid lengthy and costly court litigation.

Public Procurement and Competition Law – Expert Legal Support for Your Business

Public procurement is a highly regulated process, where municipalities and state authorities must comply with EU procurement directives and national procurement laws. Public entities are required to treat all bidders fairly, transparently, and without discrimination. However, procurement processes can sometimes lead to uncertainties and disputes, for instance, if your company’s bid has not been properly evaluated or if a procurement decision has been made in violation of regulations.

Read more

We provide expert and practical legal assistance to businesses in all matters related to public procurement and competition law. Our services include:

  • Filing a request for rectification with the contracting authority if the procurement decision has been made unlawfully or if irregularities are detected in the tendering process.
  • Market Court proceedings, if further legal action is required and a rectification request has not resolved the issue.
  • Advisory services on procurement processes, helping your business successfully participate in tenders and comply with all necessary requirements.
  • Competition law assessments, in cases where there are concerns about unfair market practices or if your business faces competition law challenges.

Navigating public procurement regulations can be complex and time-consuming. With expert legal support, you can ensure that your business’s rights are upheld and that decision-making remains transparent.

Frequently asked questions

You ask, we answer

How long does it usually take to process legal matters?

The processing time varies depending on the case, but we do our best to resolve each case as quickly as possible. We will always keep you informed about the progress of your case.

What do I need to bring with me to the meeting with the legal office?

It is a good idea to bring any relevant documents relating to your case so that we can review them together.

How often do I need to contact my law firm?

We have an obligation to contact clients whenever there is something relevant to their case to pass on, e.g., when a decision has been made, further information is required, or any other relevant matter needs to be addressed. We are, of course, at your disposal – you can contact us with questions as and when they arise.

Can I trust your law firm to keep my case confidential?

Yes – we have a professional duty to keep our conversations with clients confidential. We are fully compliant with Finnish and European codes of conduct for lawyers regarding duty of confidentiality.
